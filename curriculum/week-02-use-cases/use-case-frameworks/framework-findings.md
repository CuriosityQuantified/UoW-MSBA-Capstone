# AI Use Case Identification and Prioritization Frameworks

## Research Findings

This document compiles frameworks, methodologies, and structured approaches for identifying, evaluating, and prioritizing AI use cases within organizations.

---

### AI Value Creation Framework (10-20-70 Rule)
**Source:** BCG (Boston Consulting Group), 2024
**Summary:** BCG's framework emphasizes that successful AI transformation depends on three components: algorithms (10%), tech and data (20%), and people and processes (70%). The framework guides organizations to focus on the human and organizational aspects of AI implementation rather than just the technology, ensuring sustainable value creation from AI initiatives.
**Link:** https://www.bcg.com/capabilities/artificial-intelligence

---

### Deploy-Reshape-Invent (DRI) Framework
**Source:** BCG (Boston Consulting Group), 2024
**Summary:** A three-value-play framework for scaling predictive and generative AI. "Deploy" optimizes existing technology, "Reshape" transforms business functions, and "Invent" creates new business opportunities. The framework helps enterprises categorize AI use cases by their transformation ambition and guides end-to-end business transformation with AI.
**Link:** https://www.bcg.com/capabilities/artificial-intelligence

---

### Tech Trends 2026: AI Adoption Framework
**Source:** Deloitte, 2026
**Summary:** Deloitte's annual Tech Trends report identifies five interconnected forces shaping AI adoption: AI goes physical (robotics convergence), the agentic reality check (silicon-based workforce preparation), AI infrastructure reckoning, the great rebuild of AI-native organizations, and the AI dilemma (security vs. leverage). The framework provides a comprehensive lens for evaluating AI use cases across physical, digital, and security dimensions.
**Link:** https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/technology-management/tech-trends.html

---

### AI Business Strategy Framework
**Source:** PwC, 2025
**Summary:** PwC's framework emphasizes that AI changes the trajectory of business transformation. It categorizes AI opportunities by their potential to transform business models, redefine value streams, and create breakthrough opportunities. The framework uses a "compressed 21st century" lens where AI-driven innovation accelerates decades of progress into months, helping prioritize use cases by their transformative potential.
**Link:** https://www.pwc.com/us/en/tech-effect/ai-analytics/ai-business-strategy.html

---

### Enterprise AI Strategy Framework
**Source:** IBM Institute for Business Value, 2026
**Summary:** IBM's framework focuses on securing AI at scale with "elastic defense" approaches. It emphasizes that conventional security models fail as AI reshapes enterprises, introducing new attack surfaces. The framework guides organizations to evaluate AI use cases through a security-first lens, ensuring governance, compliance, and risk management are integrated into the prioritization process.
**Link:** https://www.ibm.com/thought-leadership/institute-business-value/

---

### AI Transformation Playbook
**Source:** Cognizant, 2025
**Summary:** Cognizant's framework organizes AI services into categories: Agentic AI (autonomous systems), AI Training Data Services, Business Intelligence and Visualization, Data Modernization, and Generative AI. It provides a modular approach for enterprises to assess their current state and identify use cases based on data readiness, AI maturity, and business impact across these capability pillars.
**Link:** https://www.cognizant.com/us/en/services/ai

---

### AI Use Case Prioritization Matrix
**Source:** Agility at Scale, 2025
**Summary:** A structured approach for enterprises to identify AI opportunities by mapping business value against implementation feasibility. The framework uses a 2x2 matrix to categorize use cases into quick wins, strategic initiatives, low-value experiments, and defer/avoid categories, providing a visual decision-making tool for portfolio prioritization.
**Link:** https://agility-at-scale.com/ai/strategy/ai-use-case-identification-and-prioritization/

---

### AI Value-Complexity Matrix
**Source:** TCS (Tata Consultancy Services), 2025
**Summary:** TCS's framework for prioritizing AI use cases evaluates opportunities based on value potential and implementation complexity. It guides enterprises to balance quick wins (high value, low complexity) with strategic bets (high value, high complexity) while deprioritizing low-value initiatives. The framework emphasizes rapid outcome delivery through AI platforms.
**Link:** https://www.tcs.com/

---

### Agentic AI Readiness Framework
**Source:** Deloitte Tech Trends, 2026
**Summary:** A framework for evaluating organizational readiness for agentic AI deployment. It identifies that only 11% of organizations have agents in production despite 38% piloting them. The framework provides criteria for assessing process redesign requirements, operational readiness, and strategic fit before scaling AI agents from pilot to production.
**Link:** https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/technology-management/tech-trends.html

---

### AI Infrastructure Strategy Framework
**Source:** Deloitte, 2026
**Summary:** This framework addresses the "AI infrastructure reckoning" where organizations must optimize compute strategy for inference economics. Token costs have dropped 280-fold in two years, but usage has exploded faster. The framework guides enterprises to evaluate AI use cases based on infrastructure requirements: cloud for elasticity, on-premises for consistency, and edge for immediacy.
**Link:** https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/technology-management/tech-trends.html

---

### AI Governance and Risk Framework
**Source:** Cognizant, 2025
**Summary:** Cognizant's framework emphasizes that traditional AI governance is insufficient for modern enterprise AI. It provides a structured approach for evaluating AI use cases through governance lenses including data privacy, AI governance, compliance, and customer data platform integration, ensuring responsible AI practices are embedded in prioritization decisions.
**Link:** https://www.cognizant.com/us/en/services/ai

---

### AI Opportunity Assessment Framework
**Source:** IBM Think, 2026
**Summary:** IBM's framework provides guidebooks for AI adoption across business functions: customer service, talent management, and finance. It helps organizations identify use cases by function-specific ROI potential, implementation readiness, and strategic alignment. The framework emphasizes becoming an expert on fundamentals before scaling to complex enterprise AI applications.
**Link:** https://www.ibm.com/think/artificial-intelligence

---

## Summary

These 12 frameworks provide a comprehensive toolkit for AI use case identification and prioritization. They cover diverse approaches including:

- **Value-complexity matrices** (BCG, TCS, Agility at Scale)
- **Transformation stage-gate processes** (BCG DRI, PwC)
- **Capability maturity models** (Cognizant, IBM)
- **Infrastructure and security-first lenses** (Deloitte, IBM IBV)
- **Functional prioritization** (IBM Think, Salesforce)
- **Readiness assessments** (Deloitte Tech Trends)

Organizations should select frameworks that align with their AI maturity, industry context, and strategic priorities.
